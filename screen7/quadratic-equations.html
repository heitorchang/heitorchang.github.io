8. Quadratic Equations

Defining the x-axis as the domain and a function on this domain

The iota function creates a list of n evenly spaced numbers, starting at start (default is 0) with a step of step (default is 1).

(iota n start step)

(iota 10)
(iota 6 -12 3)
(iota 11 -10 2)

domain will be the 360 pixels of the canvas. Then, the quadratic function \(f(x) = 0.003 x^2 + 50\) will be plotted.

(define domain (iota 360 -180))
(define (f x)
  (+ (* 0.003 (expt x 2)) 50))

(for-each pt domain (map f domain))

The quadratic formula

Given a quadratic equation \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\), the roots are given by

\[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}\]

(define (roots a b c)
  (let ((det (sqrt (- (expt b 2) (* 4 a c)))))
    (list (/ (+ (- b) det) (* 2 a))
          (/ (- (- b) det) (* 2 a)))))

Suppose we have \((x - 3)(x + 2) = 0\). Expanding, we have \(x^2 - x - 6 = 0\). Using the formula above, the roots are:

(roots 1 -1 -6)
