How to Speak

Patrick Henry Winston, MIT

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Unzc731iCUY

How to start

Do not start with a joke. The audience has to adjust itself to you.

Instead, start with empowerment promise. Tell people what they're going to know at the end of the hour that they didn't know at the beginning of the hour.

This is the reason for being here.

Example: you will know new things about speaking.

Heuristics

It's a good idea to cycle on the subject. To ensure everyone gets what you're trying to say, you're going to have to repeat yourself.

Build a fence around your idea, so it's not confused with someone else's idea.

Verbal punctuation: provide some landmark places announcing it's a good place to get back on track (if someone in the audience fogged out).

Ask a question. It can't be too obvious, nor too difficult.

Time and place

11 AM

Place: Must be well lit

Slides vs. blackboard

Slides are more effective for exposing ideas, while a blackboard is better for teaching.

  • Do not read directly from your slides
  • Be in the image
  • Keep images simple
  • Eliminate clutter

Miscellaneous

  • Humans are storytelling and symbolic animals
