Tonnarelli

Source: YouTube Academia.tv Francesca Guastella

Ingredients

  • 200 g semola rimacinata
  • 200 g flour
  • 4 eggs

Procedure

  1. Mix the flours and make a well in the center (la fontana).
  2. Beat the eggs carefully with a fork, gradually adding the flour to the eggs.
  3. When the mixture is thick, use a scraper to mix together the remaining flour.
  4. Using your hands, shape the dough into a long cylinder. Knead it horizontally, folding a portion of the cylinder sideways, and repeating. It's OK to have small pieces of dry dough.
  5. Do not overwork the dough. Shape it into a ball, then wrap with plastic film and let it rest for 30 mins.
  6. Divide the ball into two parts. Flatten a part with a rolling pin, powdering with flour so it doesn't stick to itself. Then, fold towards the center and cut into thin strips. (Note: pasta swells when cooking, so make sure they are cut thinly).
  7. Cook for 3-4 minutes in salted water.
